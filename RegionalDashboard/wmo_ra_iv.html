Key messages

Regional mean temperature for WMO RA 4 North America

The year 2022 was ranked between the 12th and 14th highest on record. The mean value for 2022 was 0.53°C relative to the 1981-2010 average (0.47-0.61°C depending on the data set used). 6 data sets were used in this assessment: Berkeley Earth (to Oct 2022), ERA5 (to Oct 2022), GISTEMP (to Oct 2022), HadCRUT5 (to Sep 2022), JRA-55 (to Oct 2022), and NOAAGlobalTemp (to Oct 2022). Relative to a 1961-1990 baseline, the mean value for 2022 was 1.09°C (1.06-1.13°C depending on the data set used). 5 data sets were used in the assessment relative to 1961-1990.

Continental trends

Global mean temperature is based on measurements made at weather stations over land and by ships and buoys over the ocean. Temperatures are typically expressed as anomalies which are temperature differences from the average for a standard period. Here, 1850-1900 is used for the global mean. Instrumental temperature records are some of the longest climate records available, with some series extending back to the 17th century.
