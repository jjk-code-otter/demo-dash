Key messages

Atmospheric concentration of carbon dioxide

In 2020, greenhouse gas mole fractions reached new highs, with globally averaged surface mole fractions of carbon dioxide (CO2) at 413.2 ± 0.2 parts per million (ppm), methane (CH4) at 1889 ± 2 parts per billion (ppb) and nitrous oxide (N2O) at 333.2 ± 0.1 ppb, respectively 149%, 262% and 123% of pre-industrial (1750) levels. Real-time data from specific locations, including Mauna Loa (Hawaii) and Kennaook/Cape Grim (Tasmania) indicate that levels of CO2, CH4 and N2O continued to increase in 2021.
